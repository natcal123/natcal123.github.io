Welcome!

This interface prompts users to create movement based performance throughout Washington Square park in New York City. There are five sets of movements to choose from. Each movement is based on the patterns of a specific insect species living throughout the park!

This is a way to deepen our connection with nature within the city and become aware of how beings navigate the world.

EXPLORE!

SPECIES:
The Painted Lady Butterfly

MOVEMENT:
WALK OR RUN IN CIRCLES AROUND A PERSON OR A CHOSEN AREA FOR 5 SECONDS

WOO! I DID IT!

BEHIND THE MOVEMENT:
These butterflies fly in circles around each other for up to 5 seconds before parting ways, to symbolize courtship.


HABITAT:
The painted lady likes eating nectar flowers including goldenrods, marigolds and everlastings. They are seen in a variety of habitats except very dense forests.

SPECIES:
Monarch Butterfly

MOVEMENT:
FIND A GRASSY SPOT CLOSE TO YOU. BEND INTO A SQUAT POSITION AND JUMP UP.

WOO! I DID IT!


BEHIND THE MOVEMENT:
Monarchs collect moisture and minerals from damp soil and gravel through a process called mudpuddling, where they sit on the ground and suck up the moisture.


HABITAT:
While breeding, monarchs can be seen in urban and suburban areas, gardens, trees, and roadsides. Larvae only eat milkweed but adults eat the sector of a variety of plants including coneflowers, goldenrod, and lilac.

MOVEMENT:
JUMP FIVE TIMES TO THE RIGHT OF WHERE YOU ARE STANDING.

BEHIND THE MOVEMENT:
These centipedes capture their prey by jumping onto them.

They live primarily under large rocks, wood piles and compost piles.

SPECIES:
The Black Swallowtail Butterfly

MOVEMENT:
MOVE IN ALL DIRECTIONS! STAY WHERE YOU ARE STANDING AND MOVE ARMS, LEGS, HEAD, AND ABDOMEN IN ANY WAY THAT YOU WOULD LIKE.

WOO! I DID IT!


BEHIND THE MOVEMENT:
This species needs to be 24 degrees celsius to fly. In order to do this, they have special behavioral patterns of moving wing position, orientation to the sun, and abdomen position just to name a few. They are always moving!


HABITAT:
They like feeding on the celery, carrot or parsley family along with citrus plants and aromatic evergreen trees and shrubs.
